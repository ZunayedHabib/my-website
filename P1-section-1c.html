Question 1:

Step 2: Find the common numerical factor
Common numerical factor: 3
The greatest common factor of 9 and 12 is 3
Step 3: Find the common variable factors
Common variable factors: x and y
Take the lowest power of each variable: x¬π and y¬π
Step 4: Determine the HCF
HCF = 3xy
Combine the numerical and variable factors
Step 5: Divide each term by the HCF

12x2y √∑ 3xy = 4x

‚ú® Final Answer: 3xy(3y + 4x) ‚ú®

Question 3:
15x2 + 42x - 9
Solution:
Step 1: Check for common factors first
15, 42, and 9 have a common factor of 3
Always look for a common factor across all terms before factorising
Step 2: Factor out the HCF
3(5x2 + 14x - 3)
Divide each term by 3
Step 3: Factorise the quadratic inside the bracket
For 5x2 + 14x - 3:
a = 5, b = 14, c = -3
Use the product and sum method: find two numbers that multiply to a √ó c = 5 √ó (-3) = -15 and add to b = 14
Step 4: Find factor pairs of -15
Possible pairs:
1 and -15 ‚Üí sum = -14 ‚úó
-1 and 15 ‚Üí sum = 14 ‚úì
We need numbers that multiply to -15 and add to 14
Step 5: Rewrite the middle term
5x2 + 14x - 3
= 5x2 - 1x + 15x - 3
Split 14x into -1x + 15x
Step 6: Factor by grouping
= x(5x - 1) + 3(5x - 1)
= (5x - 1)(x + 3)
Group first two terms and last two terms, then factor out common terms
Step 7: Write the complete factorised form
3(5x - 1)(x + 3)
Don't forget the 3 we factored out at the beginning!
Step 8: Verify by expanding
3(5x - 1)(x + 3)
= 3(5x2 + 15x - x - 3)
= 3(5x2 + 14x - 3)
= 15x2 + 42x - 9 ‚úì
Expand to verify we get the original expression
‚ú® Final Answer: 3(5x - 1)(x + 3) ‚ú®

Question 5:

Solution:
